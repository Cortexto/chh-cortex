# Paul B. Spiegel

**Email:** pbspiegel@jhu.edu  
**Org:** Johns Hopkins University — Center for Humanitarian Health (CHH)  
**Role:** Director, CHH · Aral Surmeli's DrPH advisor

## Cortex / CHH interest (2026)

Responded May 2026 to Berktuğ's Cortex outreach — strong interest in AI grant-workflow tooling for CHH programs. **Demo held 2026-07-08** (50 min, Zoom). Reactions: "hugely impressive," "really cool," "couldn't hurt to try."

**Post-demo ask:** sanitized GitHub repo, Berktuğ setup support (~half day), explore dedicated center laptop + Google Drive for proposals.

## Operational pain points (Jul 8 demo)

| Area | Constraint |
|------|------------|
| **Calendar / email** | Hopkins IT blocks AI OAuth to Outlook; must manually export iCal → paste into Claude for daily meeting prep — "killing me" |
| **Dropbox / files** | Cannot give Claude access to institutional Dropbox |
| **Multi-model workflow** | Uses Claude business + ChatGPT; cuts/pastes between tools — no shared memory |
| **Meeting memory** | High meeting volume; forgets prior grant discussions — strongly resonated with Cortex transcript-search demo |
| **Proposal load** | Center putting together many proposals; "hell of a lot of time" |

## Preferences

- **Local/laptop ownership** of institutional memory files over proprietary cloud brain
- Open to **one shared Mac** for CHH (not every personal machine initially)
- Wants eventual access to files + email + calendar; accepts Outlook integration is hard at Hopkins
- Finds **Hopkins GPT** inferior to personal Claude — uses business Claude instead
- **Proposal-first** deployment path preferred over personal calendar automation (William aligned)

## Deployment ideas raised (demo close)

1. Dedicated CHH laptop with Cortex + Google Drive sync for consolidated proposals
2. Business Claude/Cursor accounts paid through center
3. Gmail calendar invite workaround (Manny + William — "hassle" but possible)
4. MoU with HERA (William raised, Paul receptive — like IAPS model)
