## Publication & trial anchors for THIS proposal

### Primary — utilization endpoints (already in narrative ref [7])

**Sürmeli A, Yasin Y, Narla NP, et al.** Digital reminders for antenatal care in a refugee population: quasi-experimental evaluation of HERA in Türkiye. *JMIR Preprints* 97767, 2026. NCT05094518.
→ **Verified role and design:** ClinicalTrials.gov lists Aral as principal investigator. The preprint lists him as first author and reports 48 women with confirmed HERA exposure compared with a retrospective clinic cohort of 949 women. Mean ANC attendance was 2.5 versus 1.9 visits. The planned randomized trial could not be analyzed as intended because allocation and identifiers were incompletely recorded in routine operations.

→ **Tailoring hook:** This is direct precedent not only for utilization endpoints, but for the proposal's highest-risk operational method: documenting provenance, preserving identifiers, and linking digital exposure to clinic utilization without overstating causal inference. Aral should therefore own the Aim 1 operational data map and linkage-feasibility documentation, then the Aim 3 implementation protocol and fidelity assessment. Frame the finding as association and plausibility, not proof that conversational AI changes service use.
