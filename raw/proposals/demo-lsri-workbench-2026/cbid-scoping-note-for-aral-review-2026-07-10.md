# LSRI–CBID Scoping Note

**Internal only:** Aral reviews and edits this note before anything is sent to Youseph Yazdi. It does not record a CBID commitment.

## Working project hypothesis

De-identified conversational and utilization data can identify barriers to health-service use among refugee and returnee populations, and a targeted AI-supported navigation intervention designed around one priority barrier can improve navigation feasibility and service uptake.

## Why CBID

CHH will lead the scientific work: research design, ethics, humanitarian health analysis, evaluation, and publication. HERA will provide the operational platform, de-identified data interface, and bounded technical integration. CBID can fill the translational gap between research findings and a usable intervention by applying its design methodology, bioengineering perspective, and student-team model to one clearly defined problem.

This role builds on earlier conversations in which Yazdi described funded student teams working with HERA on a concrete feature, application, or new approach. The LSRI project now offers a bounded version of that opportunity.

CBID has a direct precedent for exactly this archetype: the 2024 **Ayu AI** project, an LLM assistant built by a CBID student team with **Intelehealth** (NGO digital-health partner, rural Indian telemedicine), advised by Yazdi. His 2026 *Caredesign* paper (Annals of Biomedical Engineering) formalizes needs-finding for healthcare **service** innovation in LMICs, so a navigation workflow sits inside his own current agenda. Both are worth naming in outreach. Public-source dossier: `wiki/entities/people/youseph-yazdi.md` § Public-source dossier.

## Proposed faculty role

Subject to Yazdi's interest and availability, the faculty role would include:

- design-methodology oversight;
- supervision of the CBID student contribution;
- advice on translational and engineering feasibility;
- participation in design selection and milestone reviews;
- input into the design-methods and translational sections of resulting publications.

The role would not include primary ownership of NLP, statistical analysis, IRB, or humanitarian research unless Yazdi and Shatha choose to expand it. Named co-investigator status, advisory status, effort, and compensation remain open.

## Proposed student work package

Subject to faculty and student availability, the student team would complete one bounded design cycle:

1. Synthesize CHH-approved findings into user, accessibility, and system requirements.
2. Map the patient and navigation journey around one priority utilization barrier.
3. Generate and assess intervention concepts with CHH, HERA, and Aral.
4. Prototype one HERA-compatible navigation feature or workflow.
5. Conduct supervised usability and feasibility testing under the approved ethics and partner protocol.
6. Deliver design documentation and implementation recommendations.

The proposal should not promise a service-matching workflow, referral interface, proactive guidance feature, or any other specific product before the formative analysis identifies the barrier. These are possible examples only.

## Expected outputs

- One CHH-approved design brief linking a research finding to user and system requirements.
- One patient/navigation journey map.
- A documented concept-selection process.
- One bounded HERA-compatible prototype or workflow.
- A supervised usability and feasibility findings memo.
- Design documentation and implementation recommendations for follow-on funding.

## Indicative 24-month milestones

| Period | CHH / HERA research milestone | Proposed CBID contribution |
|--------|-------------------------------|----------------------------|
| Months 1–6 | IRB, governance, de-identification, baseline measures | Faculty scoping; student mechanism and design constraints confirmed |
| Months 7–12 | Formative conversational and utilization analysis; priority barrier selected | Findings translated into design brief and journey map |
| Months 13–18 | Intervention requirements approved | Concepts generated and one bounded prototype produced |
| Months 19–24 | Feasibility and utilization assessment | Supervised usability work, iteration, documentation, and handoff |

Milestone timing remains flexible until the IRB pathway, student calendar, and project start date are confirmed.

## Constraints that remain fixed

- Shatha remains PI and CHH remains the scientific lead.
- Aral remains the funded DrPH trainee, not co-PI.
- HERA remains within the external-partner cap and does not run a new field program.
- Privacy, IRB, data governance, accessibility, multilingual usability, and low-bandwidth use are design constraints.
- No faculty title, effort, student mechanism, participant count, discipline mix, cost, facility, or prototype form is confirmed yet.
