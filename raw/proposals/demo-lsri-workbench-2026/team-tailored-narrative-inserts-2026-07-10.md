## §4 Team Capability language integrated into canonical narrative

### Lead PI: Shatha Elnakib, PhD, MPH

Assistant Research Professor and Deputy Director, Center for Humanitarian Health, JHU Bloomberg School of Public Health. Shatha's scholarship spans the scientific bridge this proposal requires: mixed-methods research on refugee integration into national health systems (*Conflict and Health*, 2024); evaluation of reproductive, maternal, newborn, and child health service delivery during conflict (*Conflict and Health*, 2020); and validation of a mobile-health mortality-risk model using national survey data (*Journal of Medical Internet Research*, 2022). She will be accountable for study design, IRB and ethics, the qualitative codebook and validation framework, mixed-methods integration, selection of the Aim 3 design target, and publication. CHH research staff will execute NLP and statistical pipelines under her scientific oversight.

**Aim ownership:** scientific PI for Aims 1–3; accountable for validation design, health-systems and humanitarian reproductive-health interpretation, evidence integration, and the decision to translate one finding.

**Career development:** a faculty-led research line in governed conversational analytics and humanitarian health evaluation.

### Trainee: Aral Surmeli, MD, MPH

DrPH candidate (advisor: Bill Weiss, CHH) and founder of HERA Digital Health. Aral is first author of the current JMIR preprint and registered principal investigator for NCT05094518, which evaluated antenatal-care reminders among Syrian refugee women at two Istanbul migrant health centers. The planned randomized design could not be analyzed as intended because allocation and identifiers were incompletely captured in routine operations. That experience directly anticipates this proposal's data-provenance and linkage risk. On this award he is a **funded trainee beneficiary, not co-PI**, leading operational data-provenance mapping, linkage-feasibility documentation, implementation protocol and fidelity assessment, and rapid-cycle learning under Shatha's supervision.

**Career development:** converts founder-operational expertise into formal implementation-science research training at JHU.

### External partner: HERA Digital Health (≤10% subaward)

De-identified data access, platform integration, and technical support only; no new field program or primary research ownership.

### Proposed CBID collaboration: Youseph Yazdi (unchanged structure)

See `cbid-scoping-note-for-aral-review-2026-07-10.md` and `yazdi-cbid-one-pager-2026-07-10.md`.
