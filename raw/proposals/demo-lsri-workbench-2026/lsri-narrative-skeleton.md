## 1. Transformative potential and impact

**Field job:** Convince committee this is bold, high-risk/high-reward — not incremental ops.

### Bullet beats

- **Problem:** Humanitarian health programs rely heavily on **survey instruments** (e.g. Kobo) that miss conversational nuance — symptom description, trust, barriers — especially in **refugee/displacement** settings.
- **Gap:** Chatbot-health literature (`verify` Nature-class citations) largely **not refugee-specific**; HERA holds de-identified conversational health data at scale **`verify` corpus metrics**.
- **Transformative claim:** First rigorous **conversational data science** program for humanitarian maternal/reproductive health — new evidence modality, not parallel clinic.
- **Impact:** Better targeting of digital health interventions; implementation-science insights for CHH programs; trainee pipeline (Aral DrPH).

### Claims needing source

- [ ] Conversation volume / languages / geographies — **`verify` HERA ops**
- [ ] Literature gap statement — **`verify` systematic skim**

## 3. Strength of approach and feasibility

**Field job:** Credible methods, ethics, timeline — RCT not required.

### Bullet beats

**Design (sketch — pick with Shatha):**

| Option | Description | Fit |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| **Observational / implementation science** | Analyze de-identified conversation corpus; code health-seeking themes; compare to survey benchmarks where available | Lower cost; fits 2-year window |
| **Mixed methods** | Quantitative NLP + qualitative CHH review | Strongest for CHH |
| **Pilot + outcomes** | Bounded maternal-health module + conversation outcome linkage | Hybrid with Fork A |

**Methods:**

- De-identification pipeline; IRB protocol through JHU **`verify` timeline**
- NLP / thematic analysis; bias and safety review
- Compute budget line (allowable per RFP)

**Proposed CBID translational design work package (scope subject to Yazdi confirmation):**

1. Translate CHH-approved findings into user, accessibility, and system requirements.
2. Map the patient/navigation journey around one priority utilization barrier.
3. Generate and assess intervention concepts with CHH, HERA, and trainee input.
4. Prototype one bounded HERA-compatible navigation feature or workflow.
5. Conduct supervised usability and feasibility testing under the approved ethics and partner protocol.
6. Deliver design documentation and implementation recommendations.

The feature form is deliberately open until formative findings identify the barrier. Examples such as service matching, referral navigation, or proactive guidance are illustrative, not promised outputs.

**Feasibility:**

- Data **already exists** in HERA ops (latent asset) — reduces field startup
- JHU hosts analysis; HERA ≤10% or in-kind partnership
- 2-year timeline with annual progress reports

### Claims needing source

- [ ] IRB precedent for similar work — **`verify`**
- [ ] De-identification method — **`verify` HERA policy**
