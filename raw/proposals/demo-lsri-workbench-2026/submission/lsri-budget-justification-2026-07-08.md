## Narrative bullets (for Excel justification column)

1. **PI effort** funds faculty oversight of a multidisciplinary humanitarian digital health study at CHH.
2. **CBID faculty participation (if confirmed)** supports design-methodology oversight, student supervision, translational feasibility, and milestone reviews. It does not replace CHH ownership of NLP, statistical analysis, or humanitarian research.
3. **Trainee line** supports DrPH dissertation activities; program values student investment.
4. **Analyst/postdoc** executes mixed-methods and NLP analysis at JHU; no large external subs beyond HERA cap.
5. **Compute** supports JHU-hosted NLP, secure analytics, and safety review.
6. **CBID student support (if confirmed)** funds a defined design cycle: requirements, journey mapping, concept generation, one bounded prototype, supervised usability work, and design documentation.
7. **Prototyping and usability** remain unallocated until the research protocol, student mechanism, and allowable-cost treatment are confirmed.
8. **HERA line (≤$30k at $300k request)** covers de-identification, platform access, integration support, and bounded technical deliverables only, not field implementation at scale.
9. **No cost-share** required per RFP.
